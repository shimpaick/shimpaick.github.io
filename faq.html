Immigration

Will you discuss your opinion of my case without charging me?
Yes. We will personally speak with you, ask questions, and give our professional opinion of your matter and the likelihood of success — at no charge and with no obligation to retain us.
How are fees structured?
Fee structures depend on the nature of the matter. Immigration cases are typically handled on a flat-fee basis. Real estate transactions are generally billed at a flat fee per closing. We will discuss all fees clearly before you engage the firm.
How long will my immigration case take?
Processing times vary widely depending on the type of application, the service center, and current government backlogs. During your consultation, we'll give you a realistic timeline based on your specific situation and current USCIS and NVC processing data.
I received a notice from USCIS. What should I do?
Do not ignore any government correspondence. Contact our office as soon as possible — many notices require a response within a strict deadline, and missing that window can negatively affect your case.

Real Estate

What is a co-op board and why does it matter in a real estate transaction?
In New York City, many apartment buildings are structured as cooperatives. When you buy a co-op apartment, you're purchasing shares in a corporation that owns the building, not real property directly. The co-op board must approve any purchase or sale, and their requirements can be extensive. We guide buyers through the entire application process.
Do I need an attorney to buy or sell real estate in New York?
While not strictly required by law, it is strongly advisable. New York real estate contracts are complex, the closing process involves many parties and documents, and issues such as title defects, co-op board requirements, and transfer taxes can have significant financial consequences. Having experienced counsel protects your interests throughout the transaction.
Can I change attorneys in the middle of my case?
Yes. It is your legal right to change attorneys at any time. If you are considering a change, contact us and we will discuss how to make the transition as smooth as possible.
Do you handle cases outside of New York?
Our real estate practice is limited to New York State. Immigration matters, however, often extend to federal agencies and immigration courts across the country — we are available to discuss your specific situation regardless of where you are located.
